The Exterior of Bruj Al Arab

Design Inspiration and Theme

The exterior design is inspired by the sail of a traditional Arabian dhow boat, symbolizing Dubai's seafaring history and innovative spirit. The architects aimed to create a striking silhouette that would be instantly recognizable.

Materials

  • Teflon-Coated Fiberglass Fabric: The sail-like structure is covered with a double membrane of Teflon-coated fiberglass fabric, which is highly durable and translucent, allowing for an impressive interplay of light.
  • Glass and Steel: The facade features a combination of glass and steel, providing both strength and a modern look.

Key Features

  • Helipad:The hotel has a helipad near the top, which extends out from the structure, creating a dramatic visual element and offering guests a unique arrival experience.
  • Skyview Bar and Restaurant: Located on the upper floors, these areas provide panoramic views of the Arabian Gulf and add to the building's allure.
  • Sail Structure: The distinctive sail shape is not just for show; it also helps reduce wind load and contributes to the building's stability.

Construction Techniques

  • Foundation: The hotel is built on a man-made island, which required extensive underwater construction work. The island is anchored to the seabed with large concrete piles.
  • Steel Framework: The steel framework forms the backbone of the structure, providing support and flexibility. It was assembled piece by piece, with each component carefully engineered to withstand the harsh marine environment.
  • Double Membrane:The double membrane of Teflon-coated fiberglass fabric was installed using a combination of tension cables and steel supports, creating the iconic sail shape.

Artistic Elements

  • Lighting: The exterior is equipped with advanced lighting systems that illuminate the building in a range of colors, creating a stunning visual display at night.
  • Water Features: The building is surrounded by water, enhancing its maritime theme and providing a sense of tranquility.
