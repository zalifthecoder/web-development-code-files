Design Inspiration and Theme

The theme of the interior design is heavily influenced by Arabian culture, blending traditional elements with modern luxury. The designers aimed to create an awe-inspiring atmosphere that reflects both Dubai's cultural heritage and its status as a global city.

Materials

The materials used in the Burj Al Arab are among the finest and most expensive. Highlights include:

  • Gold Leaf: Approximately 1,790 square meters of 24-carat gold leaf adorn various surfaces, adding a shimmering touch of luxury.
  • Marble: The hotel features 30 different types of marble, sourced from places like Brazil and Italy, including the rare Statuario marble.
  • Crystal and Glass: Crystal chandeliers and glass mosaics enhance the hotel's splendor, creating a dazzling effect.
  • Exotic Woods and Fabrics: High-quality woods and fabrics from around the world contribute to the hotel's lavish interiors.

Key Features

  • Atrium: The world's tallest atrium, soaring 180 meters, is a central feature. It is surrounded by golden columns and offers stunning views.
  • Suites: The duplex suites are decorated with lavish fabrics, ornate furnishings, and state-of-the-art technology.
  • Royal Suite: The Royal Suite epitomizes luxury, featuring a rotating bed, private cinema, and gold and marble accents.

Artistic Elements

  • Water Features: Various water features, including fountains and aquariums, add a sense of tranquility and elegance.
  • Arabesque Patterns: Intricate patterns inspired by traditional Arabian design are found throughout the hotel, adding cultural depth to the decor.
  • Custom Artwork: Unique artworks, many created by renowned artists, adorn the walls and spaces.
