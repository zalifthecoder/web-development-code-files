What is Burj Khalifa?

Burj Khalifa is the tallest building in the world. It is located in Dubai, United Arab Emirates. The building is 828 meters tall and has 163 floors. The construction of the building began in 2004 and was completed in 2010. The building is a popular tourist attraction and has an observation deck on the 148th floor.

Construction

The construction of Burj Khalifa began in 2004. The building was designed by Adrian Smith, an American architect. The building was constructed by South Korean company Samsung C&T. The construction of the building took 6 years and was completed in 2010. The total cost of the construction was about $1.5 billion.

Features

Burj Khalifa has many features that make it unique. The building has the highest observation deck in the world, located on the 148th floor. The building also has the highest swimming pool in the world, located on the 76th floor. The building has 57 elevators and 8 escalators. The building has a total of 163 floors, with the highest occupied floor being the 154th floor.
