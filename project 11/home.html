Sydney

Sydney, the radiant jewel of New South Wales, stands as the pulsating heart of Australia. Perched on the sun-drenched east coast, this dynamic metropolis cradles Sydney Harbour and unfurls its charm about 80 km (50 mi) from the glistening Pacific Ocean in the east to the rugged grandeur of the Blue Mountains in the west. From the tranquil Ku-ring-gai Chase National Park and the meandering Hawkesbury River in the north and north-west, to the verdant expanses of the Royal National Park and the thriving Macarthur in the south and south-west, Sydneyâ€™s allure is boundless. Greater Sydney is an intricate mosaic of 658 vibrant suburbs, each nestled within one of 33 local government areas. The spirited denizens, fondly referred to as "Sydneysiders," infuse the city with an unmatched vitality. As of June 2023, this urban masterpiece boasted an estimated population of 5,450,496, comprising roughly 66% of the state's residents. Bedecked with illustrious titles such as the "Emerald City" and the "Harbour City," Sydney epitomizes sparkling elegance and iconic waterscapes.
